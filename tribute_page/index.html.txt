        <h3 id="timeline-heading">Here's a timeline of Linus Torvald's life:</h3>
        <ul id="list">
          <li><strong>1969:</strong> Born on December 28 in Helsinki, Finland.</li>
    <li><strong>1987:</strong> Purchased his first computer, a Commodore VIC-20, and began programming.</li>
    <li><strong>1991:</strong> Started developing the Linux kernel as a student at the University of Helsinki; released the first version on September 17.</li>
    <li><strong>1992:</strong> Released Linux under the GNU General Public License (GPL), promoting open-source collaboration.</li>
    <li><strong>1996:</strong> The penguin mascot, Tux, was adopted as the official Linux symbol.</li>
    <li><strong>2005:</strong> Developed Git, a distributed version control system, to manage Linux kernel development.</li>
    <li><strong>2012:</strong> Awarded the Millennium Technology Prize for his creation of Linux.</li>
    <li><strong>2014:</strong> Inducted into the Computer History Museum's Hall of Fellows.</li>
    <li><strong>2018:</strong> Received the IEEE Masaru Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award.</li>
    <li><strong>Present:</strong> Continues to oversee Linux kernel development and advocates for open-source software.</li>
        </ul>
        <p id="quote"><em>"Linus Torvalds changed the way software works. Not just the code, but the culture."</em> <br>— Eric S. Raymond, Open Source Advocate and Author</p>
